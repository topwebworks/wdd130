How do we find the buyers?
Convienience and value! 1 out of 4 people in the world purchase items online. 2023 is projected to yield 6.5 trillion dollars in online sales. Let's dig into the top 3 areas to help you get a piece of those sales.
Be an expert.

First, show your expertise in your product or service field. Create Helpful blogs and articles that add solutions to help customers solve problems without sounding like a car salesman. Create quality content that surrounds your search keyword phrases...more on that below. The web content you post on social media and websites, should solve issues and create a better quality of life for your target audience. You want to talk about benefits rather than features.

Learn empathy for your customers. Include testimonials of real people you have helped. Send surveys and ask for feedback so you are addressing their needs. Look for trends in their feedback. Once you show your audience, you sincerely can help them, you build loyalty and confidence. Never try to sell. The products you do sell should have a simple purchase path of item cart > billing/shipping info > review. One click on the same page would be ideal but a purchase path usually includes an account-level shop page.

Target hungry buyers.

Secondly, target an eager audience that fits your user vision through online campaigns. Take the time to research your competitors. How can your messaging stand out from theirs? What areas and audience is not be addressed by your competitors? Start creating user personas from actual or desired customers, from background to daily life. The more detail listed, the more you figure out your campaign messaging.

Next visit some keyword search research tools, like the Google Keyword Planner. Look for trends and try searching for keyword phrases that match your targeted personas. Use those keyword phrases in all your web media from articles to video descriptions - anything that has searchable text. You may find niche keyword areas to try campaigns on. Like instead of "pet food", try "parakeet breeding tips".

Make buying easy.

And finally, make it easy and satisfying to purchase your product or services online. Free shipping is a must. Make sure your prices cover general shipping and life just became easier for both parties. Remember, you usually only have a couple of seconds to catch a visitor's attention. Most conversions happen "above the fold" or the first screen they see.

What top "hero" offer would best grab your targeted audience's attention? Low Prices? New Benefit? Free item? Video? Add a "lead form" to create an email list of interested people. Treat this list like your family; try to help and only sell when applicable. You can add jump links down your landing pages but most people stay at the top. You can have a main product and service website, and landing pages. Try to keep links to a minimum so your conversion links are immediately obvious or maybe one click away on the same page.
